Multilevel Parking Management System

Problems being faced earlier:

  1. Multiple guards at the entry and exit section of MLP(multilevel parking), and one guard for each of the floors above the ground floor.
  2. Static pricing - a fixed charge(25rs for 4 wheelers & 12rs for 2 wheelers) is taken for parking duration ranging from 0 - 4 hours. Then extra charge is taken if the owner of the vehicle exceeds the time limit (this happens at the exit section).
  3. All payments are currently being done in cash which is time taking as well as not so convenient for all vehicle owners.
  4. No system to keep track of how many cars are parked on each floor. This can lead to crowded parking (lower floors are heavily crowded, while upper floors might be empty). The guards on each floor do not work well, as a result, vehicle owners have to waste time and energy searching for space on each of the lower floors before parking on some upper floor.
  5. No system to charge fines to vehicle owners who park their vehicles improperly and cause inconvenience to other vehicle owners.
  6. No detailed collection of data on entry-exit of cars, which could be used to find out the peak hours and adopt dynamic pricing

Features introduced to solve the above problems:

  1. Only 2 guards needed - for entry/exit irrespective of the number of floors (if floors increase because of traffic load, the number of guards still remains same)
  2. Dynamic pricing model will be used by the system to calculate fares - higher fares will be taken during rush/peak hours of traffic
  3. An option to pay online via dynamic QR codes - vehicle owners only scan and fill UPI pin (amount to be paid is already fed to the QR code) - Digital Payments are promoted, time is saved, and convenient for many people
  4. The cameras at each floor will be utilised to keep track of cars at each floor. Accordingly, the system will assign floors, based on a routing algorithm, to new cars at the entry section hence will ensure sparse/ distributed parking, and will save time of vehicle owners searching for space at each floor.
  5. Since cameras are keeping track of cars on each floor, those cars parked on a floor different from the floor assigned to them will fined at exit section.
  6. A proper database is being maintained where details like car plate number, entry time, floor assigned, floor entered, payment mode, amount of fine, etc will be stored which can be analyzed to determine rush/peak hours and also for other essential information.
